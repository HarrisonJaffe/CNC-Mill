IMPORTANT NOTES BEFORE WIRING
  • SENSOR VCC: Endstop headers are 5V only. NPN sensors need 6-36V. Connect Brown wire to 24V rail directly. Leave header VCC pin unconnected.
  • DUAL POWER INPUT: Octopus Pro has two separate inputs — PWR and MOTOR_POWER. Both must be wired to your MEAN WELL PSU.
  • DIAG JUMPERS: Remove ALL DIAG jumpers before power-on. Leaving them in causes false endstop triggers.
  • AC MAINS: E-stop cuts AC Live before both PSUs. Lethal voltage — qualified persons only.

⚡ E-Stop — AC Mains

The NC mushroom button is in series with the AC Live wire before both PSUs. Pressing it kills all power simultaneously. Wire order: Wall Live → E-stop IN → E-stop OUT → junction → both PSU L terminals. Neutral and Earth bypass the E-stop directly. AC mains is lethal. Only qualified persons should make these connections.

🔴 Dual Power Input — Critical

The Octopus Pro V1.1 has two separate terminal blocks: PWR (board logic) and MOTOR_POWER (driver power). Both must be wired to your MEAN WELL PSU — run two separate pairs of wires from the PSU terminal block. Set each driver socket's motor power jumper to VIN so it routes from the MOTOR_POWER terminal.

🟡 NPN Sensor Power — Critical

Endstop headers (PG6–PG15) supply 5V only. Your NPN sensors need 6–36V. Leave the VCC pin on every endstop header unconnected. Connect each sensor's Brown wire directly to the 24V rail. Blue wire to GND. Black signal wire through a 1N4148 diode (anode→sensor, cathode→header SIG pin) to the endstop signal pin. The board's internal pull-up handles the rest.

🟠 TMC2209 Installation

Check orientation against board silkscreen notch before pressing drivers in. Stick heatsink on chip first. Insert only the single UART jumper per socket — remove the other three SPI jumpers entirely. Remove ALL DIAG jumpers across the board. Each driver has its own dedicated UART pin on the Octopus Pro — no MS1/MS2 addressing jumpers needed unlike the standard Octopus.

🟣 Spindle ESC & PWM

Spindle runs off its own PSU and ESC. The Octopus provides two signals: an on/off relay via PA8 (FAN0) and a PWM speed signal via PE5 (FAN1). You must connect Octopus GND to ESC signal GND — this shared reference is required for the PWM signal to work. Without it the ESC won't respond correctly even though the PWM is present.

⚠️ Before First Power-On

Complete jumper checklist before applying any power. Do not connect motors or loads until Klipper firmware is flashed and confirmed. Board connector labels are on the bottom — photograph it before mounting. Verify NO vs NC on your proximity sensors when they arrive and update Klipper endstop_pin accordingly. Order an X-axis stepper motor if not already done.
